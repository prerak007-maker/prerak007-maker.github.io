TVS Apache RTR 200 Fi E100

The TVS Apache RTR 200 Fi E100 is India's first and currently only ethanol-powered production motorcycle. It is priced at Rs 1.2 lakh (ex-showroom) and is available only in Karnataka, Maharashtra and Uttar Pradesh. The E100's 197.75cc single-cylinder air and oil-cooled engine uses twin-spray twin-port electronic fuel-injection technology.Final output fingers stand at 21PS at 8500rpm and 18.1Nm at 7000rpm, which is quite close to the standard fuel-injected variant. Transmission duties are handled by a 5-speed gearbox and slipper clutch. The underpinnings are the same as the petrol-powered RTR 200 4V.

TVS Apache RTR 180

For the BS6 model, TVS has fitted LED DRLs to the existing halogen bulb headlight unit. Powering the RTR 180 is the old 177.4cc single-cylinder motor but it now gets TVS’s Race-Tuned Fuel-Injection system. The new engine output stands at 16.79PS at 8500rpm and 15.5Nm at 7000rpm. The max power rating has gone up by 0.17PS while peak torque, which is the same as before, now kicks in at 500rpm higher. The engine also gets the Glide Through Traffic feature like other Apaches. Suspension duties include a telescopic fork and twin gas-charged shock setup. Anchoring the bike are disc brakes at both ends..

TVS Apache RTR 200 4V

To comply with BS6 regulations, TVS has equipped the RTR 200 4V with fuel injection. The 197.75cc, single-cylinder engine now generates 20.5PS of power at 8500rpm and 16.8Nm of torque at 7500rpm. While the power output is the same as before, the peak torque is down by 1.3Nm. It retains its 5-speed transmission and slipper clutch setup.Interestingly, the bike also comes equipped with SmartXonnect technology, a feature that was first introduced on the BS4 Apache RTR 200 4V. It also gets a new all-LED headlamp, redesigned rearview mirrors, feather touch start system, and Glide Through Traffic feature.

TVS Apache RTR 160

TVS has launched the BS6-compliant Apache RTR 160 in India. The rear disc version is priced at Rs 96,500 (ex-showroom, Delhi) while the rear drum variant retails at Rs 93,500. Both BS6 variants now feature fuel-injection. The updated BS6-compliant 159.7cc single-cylinder 2-valve air-cooled motor puts out 15.53PS at 8400rpm and 13.9Nm at 7000rpm -- that’s 0.42PS and 0.87Nm more than the BS4 model.Suspension setup remains the same as the BS4 model. It retains the same telescopic fork and monoshock setup as the older generation model. Braking is via a 270mm front and 200mm rear petal disc with a single-channel ABS offered as standard.

TVS Zeppelin

TVS’ first cruiser concept and is powered by a 220cc air-cooled motor, aided by an electric motor. The setup is similar to Maruti Ciaz’s mild-hybrid system and is claimed to help in acceleration and to save up on fuel. The engine transfers power to the rear wheel using a final belt drive. The Zeppelin concept rides on premium 41mm gold finished inverted fork and a monoshock. Brakes include a disc up front with radial caliper and a petal disc at the rear. The motorcycle rolls on spoke wheels wrapped with tubeless tyres. Other interesting features of the bike include all-LED lighting system and an HD camera that’s neatly integrated with the headlamp cowl to record your rides.
