MT-15

The Yamaha MT-15 is essentially the naked streetfighter iteration of the YZF-R15 Version 3.0. It draws power from the same engine as the R15 but the final sprocket is larger, for better acceleration than outright top speed. Like the previous version, the BS6 MT-15 gets a six-speed gearbox with slipper clutch. The delta box frame is suspended on a telescopic fork and a linked mono-shock. Brakes include 282mm front and 220mm rear disc with single-channel ABS as standard. The 17-inch alloy wheels are shod with 100-section tyre front and 140-section rear radial tyre.

Yamaha YZF R15 V3

The BS6 R15 V3.0 gets new graphics and three colour options: Racing Blue, Thunder Grey, and Darknight. It gets new features like rear radial tyre, side stand engine cut-off switch and dual horn setup. The updated BS6-compliant 155cc liquid-cooled single-cylinder motor now makes 18.6PS at 10,000rpm and 14.1Nm at 8500rpm. That is a drop of 0.7PS and 0.6Nm over the BS4 variant. The new R15 V3.0 competes with the KTM Duke 125.

MT 09

Yamaha has updated its naked street fighter, the MT-09 for 2019. The mid-capacity motorcycle gets a new white paint scheme and has received a price hike of Rs 16,000. While the frontend on the MT-09 draws its design inspiration from the bigger MT-10 with the dual LED headlights, the remainder of the design remains unchanged. The MT-09 is propelled by an 847cc, 3-cylinder, liquid-cooled, 4-stroke, DOHC, 4-valve powertrain that is linked to a 6-speed constant mesh gearbox. Peak power produced is 115PS, while peak torque generated is 87.5Nm. Other features include a slip and assist clutch, quick shifter and 3 riding modes.

Fascino

The Yamaha Fascino is powered by a 113cc air-cooled engine, which churns out 7.2PS at 7500rpm and 8.1Nm at 5000rpm, working alongside a CVT unit. Suspension bits include telescopic forks up front and a single shock absorber at the rear. Braking duties are handled by drum brakes on both ends, with UBS (Unified Braking System). The Fascino competes with the Honda Activa 5G, Hero Maestro Edge, TVS Jupiter, TVS Wego and the likes in its segment.

Yamaha YZF R3

While the current BS4 Yamaha YZF-R3 costs Rs 3.51 lakh (ex-showroom Delhi), expect the facelifted model to be priced at around Rs 3.7 lakh to Rs 3.9 lakh (ex-showroom). It will compete against the KTM RC 390, TVS Apache RR 310, Benelli 302R and Kawasaki Ninja 300.
